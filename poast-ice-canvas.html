THE KILLING OF RENÉE NICOLE GOOD
▼
RENÉE NICOLE MACKLIN GOOD
37 years old · U.S. Citizen · Mother · Wife · Minneapolis, Minnesota · January 7, 2026
The Shooting
ICE agent Jonathan Ross, 43, approached Good's vehicle during Operation Metro Surge — DHS's deployment of 2,000 agents to the Twin Cities. Good's car was stopped sideways in the street. Ross circled the vehicle on foot. An agent reached through her open window, ordering her out. Good briefly reversed, then moved forward. Ross fired three shots — first into her windshield, then at close range through the open driver's side window. Video shows Ross was standing out of the vehicle's path when he opened fire.
Autopsy Findings
Family-commissioned autopsy found three clear gunshot wounds plus a graze: one to her head (fatal), one to her left forearm, one to her right breast. The head wound killed her. U of Minnesota law professor Emmanuel Mauleon reviewed video and called it "egregious and reckless conduct" — noting Ross fired so rapidly he nearly shot his own colleague who was standing at Good's door.
DHS Use-of-Force Policy
DHS's own use-of-force policy, updated 2023, specifically discourages shooting at moving vehicles. Policy requires force to be "objectively reasonable" and states deadly force should only be used when officers face "an immediate threat of significant physical harm" after all other alternatives have been exhausted. Multiple video angles show Good's vehicle was moving away from Ross when he fired.
Medical Access Denied
Bystanders including a man who identified himself as a doctor attempted to reach Good after the shooting. ICE agents stopped them from rendering aid. Neighbors demanded immediate medical care. Firefighters eventually removed Good from the vehicle. She was transported to Hennepin County Medical Center where she later died.
Investigation Suppressed
Less than three hours after the shooting, DHS tweeted that Good had "weaponized her vehicle" and "attempt[ed] to run over our law enforcement officers in an attempt to kill them" — with no investigation whatsoever completed. President Trump publicly declared the agent did nothing wrong. Deputy AG Todd Blanche stated on Fox News Sunday the DOJ would not open a criminal investigation: "everybody can watch the videos and see that [Ross] got attacked with a car." Videos show the opposite.
